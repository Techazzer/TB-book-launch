Process & Logic Documentation

Transparency guide on how the Book Launch Intelligence Dashboard fetches, calculates, and analyzes market intelligence data.

📊 Data Definitions & Metrics

Total Addressable Market (TAM)

Logic: Extracted from "Estimated Applicants" in the Exam Schedule data (SarkariResult/Testbook sources).

The dashboard parses numerical representations of volume (e.g., "35 Lakhs" or "2 Cr") into standardized numbers to determine the potential scale of the book launch.

Best Seller Rank (BSR)

Logic: Multi-strategy extraction targeting Amazon-specific BSR fields.

We use a 3-tier fallback strategy:
1. Scrape the direct BSR label in the product details.
2. Scan the "Bestsellers" section in the product description.
3. Search for regex patterns (e.g. #123 in Books) across the full page text.

Price Gap (Best-Seller vs Avg)

Logic: Best Rated Book Price - Average Category Price.

This metric identifies if the current market leader is pricing at a premium or discount relative to the broader competitive set. Green indicates a competitive advantage (lower price), while red suggests a premium strategy.

🕷️ Universal Scraping Strategy

The dashboard now focuses exclusively on Amazon India to ensure the highest data consistency and reliability for decision making.

  1. Automated Cleanup: When a new scrape is triggered for an exam, the system automatically deletes all previous products and reviews for that exam to prevent duplicate data points.
  2. Scaling Capabilities: Users can select between 10, 20, 50, 100, or 200 products via the dashboard control bar.
  3. Inline Review Extraction: To bypass bot-detection on separate review pages, the scraper now extracts reviews inline directly from the product detail page.
    • We collect up to 5 Positive (≥4★) and 5 Negative (≤2★) reviews per book.
    • This ensures every book in the Competitor Analysis has a representative sample of reader sentiment.
  4. Attribute Extraction: Uses advanced parsing for Book Format (PYQ/Theory), Language, Publisher, and Author directly from Amazon's specification bullets.

🧠 AI Intelligence (Claude 3.5 Sonnet)

Sentiment and gap analysis is performed on-demand via the "Sentiment Check" button to optimize API costs and focus only on validated data sets.

Holistic Sentiment Analysis

Logic: The system sends all collected inline reviews for the top N products to Claude.

The AI performs semantic analysis to determine positive/neutral/negative percentages and identifies the "Top 5 Reader Praises" and "Top 5 Reader Complaints" across the entire category.

Consolidated Gap Analysis

The AI looks for recurring patterns in the "Negative" reviews across all competitors to identify market gaps:

  • Content Gaps: Missing solutions, outdated syllabus, or thin explanations.
  • Quality Gaps: Poor paper quality, binding issues, or printing errors.
  • Feature Gaps: Lack of QR code access, missing mock tests, or insufficient practice questions.

Positioning Recommendation

Based on the detected gaps and price distribution, the AI generates a specific Target Positioning recommendation, advising whether to launch as a "Premium Comprehensive Guide" or a "Budget Practice-Heavy PYQ" book.
